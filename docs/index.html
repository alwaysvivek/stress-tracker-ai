Kinematic Analysis

We analyze your mouse movements for jitter and path efficiency. High frequency tremors or inefficient, curved paths often correlate with Sympathetic Nervous System (SNS) activation‚Äîyour body's "fight or flight" response.

Keystroke Dynamics

We measure Flight Time Variability (the consistency of time between key presses). According to Epp et al. (2011), variances in typing rhythm are a strong proxy for cognitive load and mental fatigue.

Psychomotor Hesitation

By tracking Click Latency (time between stopping the mouse and clicking), we can detect "choking" or hesitation, often a sign of high performance anxiety or decision paralysis.

1. Calibration (The Baseline)

Everyone is different. First, we record a 60-second "normal" work session. This establishes your personal baseline for speed, accuracy, and movement patterns.

2. Z-Score Deviation

During live tracking, we compare current metrics to your baseline using Z-Scores (standard deviations). A Z-Score > +1.5 indicates a significant deviation (e.g., significantly more jittery than usual).

3. Clinical Agent (Llama 3.2)

We feed these Z-Scores into a local LLM agent acting as a Clinical Psychiatrist. It analyzes the combination of signals (e.g., Slow Typing + High Jitter) to distinguish between Fatigue vs. Anxiety.
