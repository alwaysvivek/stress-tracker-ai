How It Works

A simple guide to how StressTracker AI understands your stress levels.

1. The Big Idea

When you get stressed or tired, your body movements change in subtle ways. You might type with uneven rhythm or move the mouse efficiently. StressTracker AI notices these tiny changes to figure out when you need a break.

It's based on a simple rule: When your brain is overloaded, your fine motor skills get slightly worse.

2. What We Track (And Your Privacy)

The app listens to your mouse and keyboard activity in the background.

The system does NOT read what you type. It doesn't care about what keys you press (like passwords or messages). It only measures how you press them (the timing and rhythm). All data stays on your computer.

3. The Signals It Looks For

3.1 Typing Patterns

The app analyzes the rhythm of your typing.

  • Hesitation (Flight Time): How long you pause between keys. If you start pausing irregularly, it often means you're thinking hard or struggling to focus.
  • Hold Time (Dwell Time): How long you hold a key down. Longer holds can mean you're tired.
  • Mistakes: Using the Backspace or Delete key frequently suggests frustration or loss of focus.

3.2 Mouse Patterns

Your mouse movements can show if you are tense.

  • Shaky Hand (Jitter): When you are stressed, your muscles can get a bit tense, causing tiny, almost invisible shakes in the mouse cursor.
  • Winding Paths: When you're relaxed, you usually move the mouse in a straight line. When stressed, movements might become more curved or "winding."
  • Slow Clicks: If you stop moving the mouse but wait a split second before clicking, it might mean you are hesitating.

4. How the Score is Calculated

Everyone is different, so the app first learns your "normal."

4.1 Finding Your Baseline

When you use the "Calibration" mode, the app watches you work normally for 1 minute. It records your typical speed and rhythm.

4.2 The Stress Score

During a live session, the app compares your current behavior to your baseline:

  • If your movements are similar to your baseline, your stress score stays Low.
  • If you are hesitating more, making more errors, or your mouse is shakier than usual, your score goes High.

All these signals are combined into a single number from 0% (Relaxed) to 100% (High Stress).
